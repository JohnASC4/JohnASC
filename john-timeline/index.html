Shigeru Miyamoto

Shigeru Miyamoto is an iconic video game director, producer, artist, designer, and general manager.

Miyamoto's Achievments

1981
Miyamoto created the first Mario game: Donkey Kong. This game is the start of the Mario franchise.
1986
In 1986, Miyamoto and co-designer Takashi Tezuka worked on designing a combat adventure game, this game turned out to be The Legend of Zelda.
1998
In 1998, Miyamoto was honored as the first person inducted into the Academy of Interactive Arts and Sciences' Hall of Fame.
2006
Miyamoto was instrumental in developing the video game console 'Wii'.
2007
Miyamoto received the Lifetime Achievement Award "for a career that spans the creation of Donkey Kong, Super Mario Bros.
